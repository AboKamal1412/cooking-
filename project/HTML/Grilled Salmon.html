Perfect Grilled Salmon

This simple yet elegant grilled salmon recipe brings out the best flavors of fresh salmon with minimal ingredients. The salmon fillets are marinated briefly in a mixture of olive oil, lemon, and herbs before being grilled to perfection, creating a delicious meal ready in under 30 minutes.

Prep Time 10 minutes
Cook Time 12 minutes
Total Time 22 minutes
Servings

Cooking Timer

12:00

Ingredients

  • 4 salmon fillets (about 6 oz each), skin on
  • 3 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped (or 1 teaspoon dried)
  • 1 teaspoon sea salt
  • 0.5 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • 1 lemon, sliced into rounds for serving
  • Fresh herbs for garnish (dill or parsley)

Instructions

  1. In a small bowl, whisk together olive oil, lemon juice, minced garlic, dill, salt, and pepper.
  2. Place salmon fillets in a shallow dish, skin side down. Pour the marinade over the salmon, coating the flesh side well. Cover and refrigerate for 15-30 minutes (not longer or the acid in the lemon will start to cook the fish).
  3. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat (about 375째F to 400째F). Oil the grill grates well to prevent sticking.
  4. Remove salmon from marinade and place skin side down on the grill. Close the lid and cook for 6-8 minutes without moving.
  5. Once the salmon easily releases from the grill and the skin is crispy, carefully flip the fillets and cook for another 2-4 minutes, or until the internal temperature reaches 125째F for medium-rare or 130째F for medium.
  6. Transfer the grilled salmon to a serving platter, garnish with lemon slices and fresh herbs, and let rest for 3 minutes before serving.

Chef's Tips

  • For best results, bring salmon to room temperature for about 15 minutes before grilling.
  • Keep the skin on during cooking - it helps hold the fish together and adds flavor. You can easily remove it after cooking if desired.
  • Don't move the salmon too early during grilling. Let it cook until it naturally releases from the grill grates.
  • If you don't have a grill, you can use a grill pan on the stovetop with similar results.
  • For a cedar plank variation, soak a cedar plank in water for at least 1 hour, then grill the salmon on top of the plank for a delicious smoky flavor.

Serving Suggestions

This grilled salmon pairs wonderfully with:

  • Grilled asparagus or zucchini
  • Roasted baby potatoes with herbs
  • Fresh garden salad with a light vinaigrette
  • Quinoa or wild rice pilaf
  • A chilled glass of Sauvignon Blanc or Pinot Grigio
