Chocolate Lava Cake

This decadent chocolate lava cake has a rich, gooey center that flows like molten lava when cut. Perfect for special occasions or when you just want a quick, impressive dessert, these cakes are surprisingly easy to make in under 30 minutes.

Prep Time 10 minutes
Cook Time 12 minutes
Total Time 22 minutes
Servings

Cooking Timer

12:00

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup (1 stick) unsalted butter
  • 4 oz bittersweet chocolate (60–70%), chopped
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 egg yolks
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • Butter and cocoa powder for greasing ramekins
  • Powdered sugar and vanilla ice cream (optional, for serving)

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 425°F. Generously butter 4 ramekins and dust with cocoa powder. Tap out the excess.
  2. Melt butter and chocolate together in a double boiler or microwave in 30-second intervals, stirring until smooth. Let cool slightly.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, egg yolks, and sugar until thick and pale.
  4. Slowly whisk in the melted chocolate mixture. Fold in the flour until just combined.
  5. Divide the batter evenly between the ramekins and place on a baking sheet.
  6. Bake for 12–13 minutes, until the edges are firm but the center is still soft.
  7. Let rest for 1 minute, then carefully invert each cake onto a plate. Serve immediately with powdered sugar and ice cream.

Chef's Tips

  • Use high-quality chocolate for the richest flavor.
  • Don’t overbake—center should still jiggle slightly when shaken.
  • Grease ramekins well to ensure easy removal after baking.
  • Batter can be made a few hours ahead and refrigerated—bring to room temperature before baking.

Serving Suggestions

Chocolate lava cakes pair well with:

  • Vanilla bean or raspberry ice cream
  • Fresh berries
  • Whipped cream or crème fraîche
  • A glass of Port, espresso, or strong black coffee
